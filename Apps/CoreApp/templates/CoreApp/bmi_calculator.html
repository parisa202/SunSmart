What is BMI?

BMI stands for Body Mass Index. It's a measure used to assess an individual's body weight in relation to their height. It's calculated by dividing a person's weight in kilograms by the square of their height in meters. The formula is: BMI = weight (kg) / (height (m) * height (m))

For example, if a child is 130CM in height and 40KG in weight. Then the BMI of this child is 40 / (1.3 * 1.3) = 23.7

BMI is commonly used as a screening tool to categorize individuals into different weight status categories, such as underweight, normal weight, overweight, and obese. However, it's important to note that BMI is a rough estimate and doesn't directly measure body fat or overall health. It's just one of many factors to consider when evaluating an individual's health status.
